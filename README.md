# 🥦 Predicting Demand for Perishable Goods

## 📖 Project Overview
This project explores how historical sales data can be used to analyse and forecast demand for perishable goods.  
The analysis focuses on understanding sales patterns, product behaviour, and the potential influence of factors such as price, marketing spend, and wastage.

The goal is to demonstrate how data analysis can support better inventory planning, reduce waste, and improve supply chain decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyse sales trends across products and product categories
- Explore relationships between price, marketing spend, and sales performance
- Identify patterns that influence demand for perishable goods
- Build exploratory models to support demand forecasting
- Visualise insights to support data-driven decision making

---

## 📂 Data Description
The dataset includes structured product-level sales data with variables that support demand analysis.

Key fields include:

- `Product_ID` — unique identifier for each product  
- `Product_Category` — product classification or category  
- `Units_Sold` — number of units sold during a given time period  
- `Wastage_Units` — quantity of products wasted or unsold  
- `Price` — average price per unit  
- `Marketing_Spend` — marketing investment associated with a product or category

These variables enable analysis of sales performance, demand patterns, and potential drivers of product demand.
